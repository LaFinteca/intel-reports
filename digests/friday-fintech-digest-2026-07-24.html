Ant International closes the biggest round of 2026.

Cross-border infrastructure just became fintech's most contested prize, and Ant Group's global arm got there first.

GLOBAL · FUNDINGMEGA ROUND

Ant International Raises ~$1.2B Series A at $10B+ Valuation

Ant Group's global arm closed its first-ever external funding round, with Alibaba and Ant Group anchoring the raise. The capital connects 150M+ merchants to 2B digital accounts worldwide and targets cross-border payments, agentic-commerce tooling, and global account services for SMBs. It is the largest fintech funding round of 2026 so far, and the clearest signal yet that infrastructure, not consumer apps, is where growth capital wants to sit.

→ BusinessWire · 21 Jul 2026

Signal

If Ant International's rails become the default cross-border layer for 150M+ merchants, every regional PSP faces the same question Stripe posed to PayPal last week: build the infrastructure, or rent it from whoever already owns 2B accounts.

Infrastructure is eating the stack.

This week's biggest signals (Ant International's $1.2B to own cross-border rails, Augustus building a global dollar bank at unicorn scale, Núclea acquiring fraud intelligence) all point the same direction: the money is flowing to whoever owns the pipes. Consumer-facing brands still grab headlines, but the infrastructure layer is where capital concentrates and margins compound. For PSPs in LATAM, the message is clear: integrate deeper into the plumbing or risk being a reseller on someone else's rails.
