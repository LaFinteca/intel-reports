GLOBAL M&A

Stripe and Advent Launch $53 Billion Takeover Bid for PayPal

Stripe and Advent International have submitted a joint offer of $60.50 per share to acquire PayPal Holdings, valuing the company at more than $53 billion. The deal carries approximately $50 billion in committed bank financing and represents a 28% premium over PayPal's Tuesday close. The proposal calls for equal ownership between Stripe and Advent with no plans to dismantle the company. PayPal's board will meet as early as July 20 to discuss. If completed, it would be the largest fintech acquisition ever and a rare case of a venture-backed company buying an S&P 500 constituent.

Payments infrastructure is the week's thesis.

From Stripe's audacious PayPal bid to Visa's stablecoin platform and Circle's bank charter, the message is consistent: the companies controlling money movement rails are consolidating power. In LATAM, Nubank's Mexico bank license and Brazil's iGaming enforcement phase signal that regulated infrastructure, whether in fintech or gambling, is becoming the defining competitive advantage. The convergence of fiat and on-chain settlement is no longer theoretical.
