Critical signal — simultaneous collapse, shared fingerprint

Katfile lost 94% of traffic between August and October 2025. Mexa.sh lost 97% between October and December 2025. Near-identical timing, near-identical referral fingerprints (both dominated by anime-sharing.com and the same hentai/JAV cluster), and Mexa.sh's crypto-exchange-heavy outgoing profile (BTSE 24.2%, Bitmart 10.3%, OrangeX 8.9%) are three independent signals pointing at a shared operator, shared PSP, or coordinated enforcement event behind both collapses.

What to flag when this gets cited

  • Traffic figures: Similarweb panel-modelled estimates, ±15% at domain level. Collapse events, forum concentration, payment-destination fingerprints, geography, and ad network data are directionally robust. Individual percentages are indicative.
  • Operator identities: Only DStorage SAS (1Fichier) and Ultranex Ltd (Rapidgator) confirmed at registry level. FileJoker/Nitroflare/Katfile/Mexa.sh operator attributions are community-sourced or inferred. The Katfile/Mexa.sh shared-operator hypothesis remains circumstantial — though fikper.com's affinity scores add weight to it.
  • FileJoker ad network: Traffichaus appeared at 2.8% aggregate but was concentrated in February 2026 (70.6% that month) before reverting. May indicate a test campaign rather than a core relationship. TrafficStars hypothesis is rejected by the data.
  • Adult similar sites: Similarweb "industry" categorisation assigns Adult to domains based on audience behaviour signals. bunkr.cr (rank #287) and filester.me (rank #2,058) carry the Adult tag — their actual content policies and traffic compositions should be independently verified before citing as adult-specific platforms.
  • 1Fichier keyword data: Volume figures are Similarweb estimates, not Search Console data. The 100% branded ratio and absence of content-category non-branded keywords are confirmed signals; exact click volumes are indicative.
  • Client identification: Referral data identifies traffic relationships, not contracts. "Client" means content-embedding partner whose link-posting generates premium conversions — a traffic relationship, not a B2B agreement.
  • Nitroflare Stripe/PayPal: Outgoing destinations confirm routing to Stripe and PayPal checkout pages. Does not confirm a merchant account in current good standing with either processor.
