Business context.

What Praxis is, how it makes money, and the three numbers that frame the opportunity. Read this before the data.

The three things to remember.

If the working session only covers three lines about Praxis, these are the three. Everything else in this document supports them.

01

Software, never funds. The license gap is the wedge.

Praxis states in capitals on its own site that its services exclude transaction processing and handling of client funds. It holds PCI DSS Level 1 and ISO 27001, and no acquiring, EMI, or PI license anywhere.

Its LATAM coverage is only as deep as the licensed PSPs it routes to. LaFinteca holds exactly the licenses Praxis must rent.

02

praxispay.com is the goldmine, praxis.tech is the brochure.

The corporate site does ~7K to 16K visits a month and reveals little.

The operational cashier praxispay.com averaged ~504K monthly visits over the trailing year. Its referral graph exposes the ecosystem from both ends: incoming traffic names the merchants, outgoing traffic names the PSPs transactions actually route to.

03

LATAM push under way. No LATAM PSP publicly named.

PIX Cascading launched October 2024, Praxis Brazil legal entity established December 2025, Peru MINCETUR registration and PAGCOR listing alongside.

Live LATAM routing visibly leans on PayRetailers, Mercado Pago, and Pagsmile. None is an announced partner. The slot is contested and open, and the window is now.

Oct 2024LATAM

PIX Cascading launches

Industry-first feature retrying failed PIX transactions across multiple PSPs. A single endpoint requests a PIX QR code from multiple providers; if one fails, the next is tried automatically. Structurally, the feature gets better with every additional PIX-capable PSP in the hub. Also on the Praxis blog.

Analyzes per-PSP performance and suggests routing changes to lift first-time approvals. Same architectural logic as cascading: the more PSPs in rotation, the better it works.

What could change the plan.

Two structural risks to the thesis, four watch-items to monitor. None blocks the merchant-conversion play outright, and each has a response.

Structural · Routing

Praxis steers merchants to a preferred LATAM PSP

If Praxis deepens an exclusive or preferred arrangement with PayRetailers, dLocal, or another regional PSP and routes merchant volume to it by default, the conversion path narrows. Response: lead with merchants whose routing is still contested, and differentiate on niche depth (PIX reconciliation, settlement-speed SLAs, FX visibility).

Structural · Competitor

Praxis vertically integrates into licensed processing

A bootstrapped orchestrator acquiring or licensing its own PSP would change the relationship overnight. The Praxis Brazil entity bears watching for exactly this: monitor it for BCB authorization filings.

Watch · Merchants

Merchant routing locked to incumbent PSPs

If a target merchant signals its LATAM flows are contractually committed to PayRetailers or Mercado Pago, shift the pitch from primary rail to redundancy rail. The platform's own cascading architecture legitimises the multi-PSP argument: redundancy is its selling point.

Watch · Category

Orchestration commoditizes

IXOPAY post-merger, Corefy's no-lock-in positioning, and Nuvei/Solidgate bundling orchestration with acquiring all compress the category. If merchants consolidate onto acquirer-owned orchestration, the addressable set shifts. Direct merchant relationships are the hedge.

Watch · Reputational

High-risk corridor diligence

Trustpilot at 2.4/5 (thin sample, mostly end-user bank-statement confusion) and one unverified scam-allegation page. Neither is material, but both will surface in any partner-bank KYB. LaFinteca's regulated posture is the differentiator here, same logic as the Easygo file.

Watch · Data

The inference base could shift under us

The merchant map rests on referral traffic that fluctuates monthly. A merchant migrating off Praxis, or Praxis moving cashier domains, would silently invalidate rows. Re-pull the Similarweb referral set quarterly before any campaign launch built on it.

  • Inferred is never confirmed: only Codere, Stake, BOSS., Delasport, SoftSwiss, Choise.com, IC Markets, and Modulus are publicly confirmed clients or partners. Every other merchant linkage rests on referral-traffic analysis and indicates payment-flow association, never a contract.
